Santa Claus (Saint Nicholas) is thought of mainly as the jolly man in red who brings toys to children on Christmas Eve.



The real Santa Claus is inspired by monk named St. Nicholas, living near Myra in Turkey back in 280 A.D. He was known for giving away all of his inherited wealth helping the poor and sick.

The name Santa Claus evolved from Nicholas' Dutch nickname, Sinter Klaas. End of 18th century a New York newspaper reported that groups of Dutch families had gathered to honor the anniversary of his death. In the early 1890s, the Salvation Army needed money to pay for the free Christmas meals they provided to needy families. They began dressing up men in Santa Claus suits and sending them into the streets of New York to solicit donations.

About the legend


Bing Crosby famously dreamt of it: a white Christmas.



However, for most parts of the country, Christmas is only at the beginning of the period when it's likely to snow. It is more likeley to see snow between January and March than in December. White Christmas was more frequent before the change of calendar in 1752 which brought Christmas Day back by 12 days. Climate change has also brought higher average temperatures and this generally reduced the chances of a white Christmas. The forecast can accurately measure if snow is likely on Christmas Day up to five days beforehand.
